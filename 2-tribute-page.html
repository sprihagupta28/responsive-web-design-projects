David Bowie

1947 – 2016 · The chameleon of rock, forever reinventing sound and self

David Bowie performing in Chicago, 2002. Photo by Adam Bielawski (Wikimedia Commons).

A life in eras

  • 1947Born David Robert Jones in Brixton, London.
  • 1969Breaks through with "Space Oddity," released days before the Apollo 11 landing.
  • 1972Introduces the flame-haired alien rock star Ziggy Stardust, redefining glam rock and stage persona.
  • 1977Records the "Berlin Trilogy" with Brian Eno, pioneering ambient and electronic textures in rock.
  • 1983Reaches global pop superstardom with the album Let's Dance.
  • 1996Inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
  • 2016Releases the album Blackstar on his 69th birthday, two days before his death — a final work widely read as a farewell.

Why he mattered

Across five decades, David Bowie treated identity itself as an art form. He moved fearlessly between glam, soul, electronic, and pop, dragging audiences into new territory each time and proving that a musician could be a shapeshifter rather than a brand. His willingness to embrace theatricality, androgyny, and constant reinvention gave permission to generations of artists and outsiders to define themselves on their own terms.

Beyond the music, Bowie was a curious collaborator — an actor, painter, and early champion of the internet as a creative and commercial medium for artists. He remains one of the most influential and best-selling musicians in history, with an estimated 100 million records sold worldwide.
