Shreya Ghoshal

Born 1984 · The voice of a generation of Indian playback music

Shreya Ghoshal at the Behindwoods Gold Icons event, 2023. Photo via Wikimedia Commons.

A career in milestones

  • 1984Born on 12 March in Berhampore, West Bengal, India.
  • 2000At sixteen, wins the television singing show Sa Re Ga Ma and is noticed by filmmaker Sanjay Leela Bhansali's family.
  • 2002Makes her Bollywood playback debut with Bhansali's Devdas, winning a National Film Award for Best Female Playback Singer and a Filmfare Award — all before turning twenty.
  • 2000sBecomes one of the most prolific and sought-after playback singers in India, recording across Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi and many more languages.
  • 2017Becomes the first Indian singer to have a wax figure displayed at the Madame Tussauds museum in Delhi.
  • TodayHolds five National Film Awards, seven Filmfare Awards, ten Filmfare Awards South and dozens of other honours, and continues to record and tour worldwide.

Why she matters

Noted for her wide vocal range and remarkable versatility, Shreya Ghoshal moves effortlessly between classical, filmi, ghazal, bhajan, and contemporary pop, and sings fluently in dozens of languages. For millions of listeners across India and its diaspora, her voice is woven into the soundtrack of everyday life — weddings, festivals, road trips, and quiet late nights alike.

More than two decades into her career, she remains a benchmark for young singers: proof that technical mastery and genuine emotional warmth can live in the same voice. Her longevity, range, and cross-language reach have made her one of the most influential playback singers of her generation.
